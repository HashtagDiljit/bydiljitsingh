Thin Plate with Hole — CFD Study (ANSYS Fluent)

Steady-state CFD of flow through a thin plate with a circular hole to quantify pressure drop, velocity amplification in the ligament, and recirculation downstream. Includes a mesh-independence study and validation against correlations.

Solo project Role: CFD Analyst Duration: [insert dates] Tools: Ansys Fluent, ParaView, MATLAB

Context & Challenge

Perforated plates and orifices appear in filters, exhausts and HVAC. Predicting loss coefficients and flow topology requires adequate near-wall resolution around the hole edge and shear layers. The challenge is to capture separation and jet contraction with a mesh that is fast enough for parametric runs.

Objectives & KPIs

Δp & K

Predict pressure drop and loss coefficient within ±5% of reference.

Mesh Independence

Converge Δp and Vmax by <1% change between refinements.

Flow Features

Resolve separation bubble length and vena contracta position.

Runtime

Keep steady solve < 60 s on desktop; residuals < 1e-5.

My Role & Method

I created the CAD, built inlet/outlet extensions, applied boundary conditions, selected a turbulence model, ran a mesh-independence study, and validated the loss coefficient against correlations. Post-processing was done in ParaView and MATLAB.

“Right where the jet contracts is where the mesh must be richest — resolve it once, then vary the parameters with confidence.”

  • 3 mesh levels → <1% change in Δp
  • Residuals to 1e-5; mass imbalance < 0.1%
  • Automated plots for Δp, Vmax, separation length

Technical Contributions

Geometry & Boundary Conditions

Thin plate with a circular orifice (diameter D), upstream and downstream ducts (≥10D) to stabilise the flow. Velocity inlet (or total pressure), pressure outlet, no-slip walls.

Domain layout and boundary conditions.

Meshing Strategy

Hex-prism inflation around the hole edge to capture shear; progressive refinement in the jet core. Three meshes: coarse, medium, fine. y+ targeted for the chosen model.

Inflation layers at the hole edge.

Turbulence Model & Numerics

RANS steady solver; SST k–ω (robust near separation) with second-order schemes. Tight pressure–velocity coupling and double precision. Convergence by residuals and Δp flat-lining.

Residuals and monitor histories.

Validation & Sensitivity

Loss coefficient compared with orifice/plate correlations; sensitivity to inlet Reynolds number and plate thickness checked. Agreement within target bounds.

Correlation vs. CFD (Δp / ½ρV²).

Reflections & Learnings

  • Local refinement at the edge and in the jet core is more impactful than uniform refinement.
  • SST k–ω gave stable separation prediction; model choice should match physics.
  • Validating Δp against correlations catches subtle BC or mesh issues early.
  • Next: transient run to examine vortex shedding and acoustic implications.
