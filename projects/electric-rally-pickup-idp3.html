Electric Rally Pickup (IDP3)

Suspension, steering, braking, tyres & vehicle dynamics for a Dakar-ready electric Toyota Hilux.

Summary (TL;DR)

Role: Coordinator • Suspension Team of 8 University project

Problem

Prove an EV pickup can survive Dakar-style stages with stability, braking reliability, and serviceable hardware.

What I did

Set vehicle-dynamics targets, led double-wishbone geometry & packaging, collaborated on EHPS steering, brake sizing & FEA, tyre/wheel spec, and integration.

Result

Design meets targets with safety factors on critical arms; brake FEA well under material limits; tyre/wheel package validated for low-pressure stages.

Key specs

Wheel travel 350 mm, wheelbase 3000 mm, track ~1800 mm, ride height 325 mm. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Braking (calc)

Decel up to ~7.7 m·s⁻² ideal; ~5.7 m·s⁻² incl. aero/rolling at 204 km/h; stopping from 204 km/h ~280 m. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Materials

Arms in AISI 4130; knuckles in AISI 4340. Peak arm stress ~1.07 GPa under worst-case, below yield. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Case Study

Goal & Context

Design the suspension, steering, braking, tyres and vehicle-dynamics package for an electric Toyota Hilux capable of the 2028 Dakar Rally — balancing stability and serviceability with the realities of a ~2,182 kg EV and a ~1,200 kg centrally-mounted battery. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

My Responsibilities

  • Defined ride/roll/frequency targets; led the double-wishbone suspension concept & kinematics.
  • Worked with the steering sub-team on EHPS (120 bar) rack selection and assist calculations. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • Co-developed brake sizing, bias and component stack; supported brake-disc thermal/structural FEA. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Specified tyres/wheels (37×12.5 R17, beadlock) for sand/rock stages and low-pressure operation. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • Led integration with chassis/powertrain teams for packaging and weight distribution (target 50:50). :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
Vehicle Dynamics Targets

Targets focused on predictable handling on mixed terrain: natural frequency ≈ 2 Hz sprung, lateral accel 1.2–1.5g (concept-level). Geometry and travel were set to preserve contact and absorb large vertical impacts. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Suspension Concept & Kinematics

Double wishbone front and rear, tuned for 350 mm wheel travel and 325 mm ride height. Arms in AISI 4130; knuckles in AISI 4340 to balance stiffness, fatigue resistance and manufacturability. FEA on the upper wishbone indicated peak equivalent stress ~1.07 GPa at worst-case dynamic load, remaining below material yield; static case ~72 MPa. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Steering

Electro-hydraulic assist (EHPS) with a heavy-duty off-road rack; calc’d rack force ~19.3 kN at 120 bar and pinion torque ~328 Nm, allowing quick ratios with manageable wheel effort. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Braking

Front 6-piston / rear 4-piston with vented two-piece rotors (380 mm F / 355 mm R). Thermal-structural FEA on the front rotor (600 °C, peak scenario) yielded ~20 MPa equivalent stress and ~1.35 mm max deformation — well under material limits. Sizing calculations project decel up to ~7.7 m·s⁻² in ideal conditions. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Tyres & Wheels

BFGoodrich All-Terrain T/A KDR2+ (37×12.5 R17) on 17×9 beadlocks for secure low-pressure running (sand 17–19 psi; gravel/rocks higher), with clearance for large rotors and callipers. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Outcome

  • Package meets Dakar-style performance and serviceability goals with component choices and safety factors validated by FEA. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}
  • Design shows viable braking performance at rally speeds with thermal margin and adjustable bias. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}
  • Tyre/wheel spec supports traction and bead retention at low pressures across stages. :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}
