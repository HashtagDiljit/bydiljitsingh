Electric Rally Pickup (Toyota Hilux EV) for the 2028 Dakar Rally

Team & Structure

30-person multidisciplinary project split into 6 specialist groups (Chassis/Impact & Cooling; Aero/Body; Vehicle Dynamics; Powertrain/Battery; Electrical & Controls; Civil/Facility).

My Role

Elected Team Lead and Group Lead. I coordinated timelines, cross-group integration, and supervisor liaison.

Brief

Build for either high speed or high efficiency. We proposed a Toyota-backed conversion of the Hilux to a fully electric rally truck for Dakar 2028 with a report, presentation and part model.

Outcome

  • Full rally setup conversion
  • Complete business plan and risk assessment
  • Modified suspension design with calculations and model
  • Sustainability considerations and implications

My responsibilities

  • Coordinating and integrating all groups to ensure a cohesive final design.
  • Modelling the double-wishbone suspension based on calculations.
  • Organising and conducting group/team/supervisor meetings to ensure everyone's up to speed.
  • Calculating vehicle dynamics specifications and conducting risk assessment.
  • Coordinating team presentation and the team-level report.
Vehicle Dynamics Targets

Predictable handling on mixed loose terrain with ~2 Hz sprung natural frequency and ~1.2–1.5 g lateral capability at concept level. Geometry and travel preserve contact over large vertical impacts.

Suspension Concept & Kinematics
  • Front & rear double-wishbone layout for durability and serviceability.
  • Travel budget and motion ratios chosen for damper headroom at rally speeds.
  • Packaging clearance around battery, cooling, steering and upright.
Steering
  • EHPS selection and assist sizing for off-road loads.
  • Rack travel vs. steering angle and stability at speed.
Braking
  • Disc & pad sizing; bias map; fade margin from the thermal model.
  • Integration with regenerative strategies for efficiency and stability.
Tyres & Wheels
  • Beadlock wheel to maintain bead at low pressures over rocks/sand.
  • Tread & compound suitable for mixed stages; serviceability focus.

Outcome

  • Package meets Dakar-style performance with serviceable component choices; targets validated by preliminary calcs and FEA checks.
  • Braking performance viable at rally speeds with thermal margin and adjustable bias.
  • Tyre/wheel spec supports traction and bead retention for low-pressure stages.
