Electric Rally Pickup — Toyota Hilux (IDP3)

Led a 30-person multidisciplinary team to design a fully electric rally truck for the Dakar Rally, converting Toyota’s Hilux into a high-speed, sustainable EV.

Team size: 30 Role: Team Leader + Mechanical Subgroup Lead Duration: Sept–May Tools: Fusion 360, Ansys Fluent, Granta Edupack

Context & Challenge

The Dakar Rally is one of the toughest off-road events on earth. Rally contenders have traditionally been ICE-powered; our challenge was to electrify a Hilux for a 2028 Dakar entry using Toyota’s next-gen solid-state battery. Beyond performance, the brief served a broader sustainability goal: prove that EVs can survive extreme endurance racing while staying fast, safe, and serviceable.

Objectives & KPIs

Performance

0–100 < 6 s, top speed > 200 km/h.

Durability

Survive 500–800 km rally stages with extreme loads.

Safety

FIA roll cage compliance, crashworthiness, HV battery safety.

Sustainability

Solid-state battery, regenerative suspension, zero tailpipe emissions.

My Role & Leadership

I was elected Team Leader for the 30-person cohort. I chaired weekly stand-ups, resolved cross-group conflicts, and kept interfaces aligned across six sub-teams. I authored shared documentation and review standards to keep design quality consistent. Within the Mechanical subgroup (suspension, steering, braking, tyres, vehicle dynamics), I contributed CAD/kinematics, vehicle-dynamics calculations, risk assessment and integration.

“I balanced technical design with team leadership, ensuring 6 subgroups integrated into one working concept.”

  • 30 teammates across 6 specialist groups coordinated
  • Delivered 3 reports & 1 final presentation on time
  • Maintained subsystem integration with 0 missed deadlines

Technical Contributions

Suspension

Double wishbone front and rear with 350 mm travel, tuned to around 2 Hz ride frequency for predictable handling on loose terrain. We explored regenerative dampers targeting ~2–5 kW per wheel in heavy compression events.

Kinematics/packaging study for long-travel double wishbone.

Steering

Electro-hydraulic power steering rack sized for off-road loads, capable of >11,500 Nm wheel torque. Ratios chosen to balance quick inputs at speed with stability and feedback.

CAD for EHPS steering rack, pump, and controller

Braking

Brembo 6-piston front / 4-piston rear with vented rotors; deceleration target 7.7 m·s⁻². Thermal/structural FEA confirmed margin for repeated high-speed stops.

Thermal/structural FEA snapshot of front rotor.

Tyres

BFGoodrich Dakar All-Terrain on beadlock wheels for low-pressure running in sand/rock, balancing endurance, puncture resistance, and grip.

Tyre & beadlock selection for low-pressure stages.

Vehicle Dynamics

Mass distribution targeted at 50:50, including a central ~1,200 kg battery pack. Stability analysis and envelope trade-offs performed with MATLAB & GP100.
