Project • IDP3

Electric Rally Pickup (Toyota Hilux EV) for the 2028 Dakar Rally

Team & Structure

30-person multidisciplinary team split into 6 specialist groups (Chassis/Impact & Cooling; Aero/Body; Vehicle Dynamics; Powertrain/Battery; Electrical & Controls; Civil/Facility).

My Role

Elected Team Lead for the full programme and my MEng subgroup (Vehicle Dynamics). I coordinated timelines, cross-group integration, and supervisor liaison.

Brief

Design a high-speed OR high-efficiency vehicle. We chose a Toyota Hilux conversion to a fully electric rally truck that showcases Toyota’s forthcoming battery technology at Dakar 2028.

Outcome

  • Defined ride/roll/frequency targets
  • Double-wishbone concept & kinematics
  • Brake sizing & thermal model
  • Tyre spec for sand/rock, low-pressure bead retention

Summary

We converted a production Hilux into a fully electric rally platform aimed at Dakar-style endurance. As Team Lead I kept the six groups aligned on interfaces (mounting points, loads, aero envelopes, electrical safety, packaging) and drove vehicle dynamics decisions across suspension, steering, braking and tyres to meet a predictable handling target on mixed terrain.

Selected visuals from my group

My responsibilities

  • Defined ride, roll and frequency targets; led double-wishbone concept and early kinematics.
  • Co-led steering system selection (EHPS 120 bar) and high-level rack ratio trade-offs.
  • Developed brake sizing & bias; produced thermal model for rally stages; component stack selection.
  • Specified tyre/wheel package (37×12.5 R17, beadlock) for low-pressure operation on sand/rock.
  • Integration lead: packaging with chassis/powertrain (battery & mass distribution towards 50:50).
Vehicle Dynamics Targets

Predictable handling on mixed loose terrain with ~2 Hz sprung natural frequency and lateral acceleration in the ~1.2–1.5 g concept range. Geometry and travel set to preserve contact over large vertical impacts.

Suspension Concept & Kinematics
  • Front & rear double-wishbone layout for durability and serviceability.
  • Travel budget and motion ratios chosen for damper headroom at rally speeds.
  • Packaging: clearance around battery, cooling, steering and upright.
Steering
  • EHPS selection and assist sizing for off-road loads.
  • Rack travel vs. steering angle and stability at speed.
Braking
  • Disc & pad sizing; bias map; fade margin from thermal model.
  • Integration with regenerative strategies for efficiency and stability.
Tyres & Wheels
  • Beadlock wheel to maintain bead at low pressures over rocks/sand.
  • Tread & compound suitable for mixed stages; serviceability focus.

Outcome

  • Package meets Dakar-style performance with serviceable component choices; targets validated through preliminary calcs and FEA checks.
  • Braking performance is viable at rally speeds with thermal margin and adjustable bias.
  • Tyre/wheel spec supports traction and bead retention for low-pressure stages.
