Wooden Bridge — IDP 1B (Jelutong)

Student-budget bridge built from Jelutong sheet stock with standard fasteners. I owned CAD modelling, the Bill of Materials, and contributed to concept/design decisions.

Team size: 5–6 Role: CAD & Bill of Materials Duration: Feb–Mar 2023 Tools: Fusion 360, Hand Tools, Drill Press

Context & Challenge

Design and build a wooden bridge from student-accessible materials within a strict budget. The structure had to be cut from 600×600×10 mm Jelutong sheets, assembled with standard screws/bolts, and fit on a bench-top test rig while meeting strength and stability requirements.

Objectives & KPIs

Budget

Keep total material cost ≈ £4.80.

Cut Plan

Stay within 5630 mm total timber length (all pieces).

Footprint

Assembly envelope about 520×390 mm.

Safety

Workshop build with risk controls & PPE in place.

My Role & Contributions

I produced the CAD model & drawings, compiled the Bill of Materials, and contributed to concept choices that balanced cut lengths, fixings and stability. I also supported workshop preparation by aligning the cut plan with the risk controls in our build method statement.

“I kept the bridge buildable on a student budget — clear drawings, a tight BOM, and a clean cut list.”

  • 1 CAD pack delivered (assembly + hole/fastener callouts)
  • £4.80 total material cost maintained
  • Cut list summed to ~5630 mm; stock plan fit on 600×600 mm sheets

Technical Contributions

Bill of Materials

Jelutong wood sheets 600×600×10 mm (×12); self-tapping wood screws No.6, 3.5×19 mm (×44); screws & nuts M4×30 mm (×4). Estimated total cost ≈ £4.80.

BOM snapshot from the assignment pack.

Cut Plan

Standardised member lengths (e.g., 520×2, 295×4, 220×4 … 230×2) summing to **~5630 mm** total timber. This kept waste low and assembly steps repeatable.

Cut lengths & quantities for all members.

CAD & Drawing Pack

Assembly envelope about 520×390 mm; hole callouts for M4 fixings and Ø4 pilot holes; chamfers and countersinks called up where needed for flush fasteners.

Assembly drawing with critical dimensions & callouts.

Workshop Risk Controls

Mitigations for fire, trips, electrocution, machine entrapment, eye/back injury; PAT-tested tools, PPE (goggles/boots), clear e-stops and signage; guarded machines and tidy walkways.

Risk assessment & residual risk ratings.

Reflections & Learnings

  • Clear drawings and a tidy BOM de-risk student builds under time pressure.
  • Normalising cut lengths reduces waste and speeds assembly.
  • Pre-agreed workshop controls (PPE, e-stops, signage) keep the build smooth.
