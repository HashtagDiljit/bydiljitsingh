Green Hydrogen Plant (IDP2) — Concept & Detailed Design

Led the mechanical design for a 6-person team, delivering concept and detailed design of a green hydrogen facility near Melbourne, powered by renewable electricity and targeting ≥14,000 kg/day H₂ with carbon-zero operation.

Team size: 6 Role: Group Coordinator, Mechanical Design Duration: Feb–Apr 2024 Tools: Fusion 360, Ansys Fluent, Granta Edupack

Context & Challenge

Design a scalable, carbon-zero green hydrogen facility to supply your given country (Australia). The site is situated by the Patterson River to secure process water, with a renewable electricity supply and space for future expansion. The core challenge was to meet a production target of ≥14,000 kg/day H₂ while integrating safe operations, high-purity gas separation, and practical storage/dispatch.

Objectives & KPIs

Production

Target ≥ 14,000 kg/day hydrogen with 24/7 runtime.

Purity

Gas separation to ~99.6% H₂ (PSA), compliant with end-use specs.

Safety

PFD/P&ID with interlocks, relief & emergency shutdown; storage up to 350 bar.

Sustainability

Renewable power (hydro/solar), water treatment & recycling, carbon-zero operations.

My Role & Contributions

As Group Coordinator for the team, I led coordination across sub-units and delivered key technical work: Feedwater Design, PDS, and CAD for the overall facility. I also contributed to the concept-stage business case, as well as risk & sustainability work.

  • 6 teammates coordinated across electrolysis, separation, storage & integration
  • Delivered 2 reports (concept + detailed), 1 PFD & 1 P&ID
  • Maintained interface control - zero missed deadlines in the mechanical workstream

Reflections & Learnings

  • You have to clearly set out the rules and roles by strengths and weaknesses so everyone knows what they're doing.
  • Helping resolve issues in good time helps keep motivation up and research going well.
  • Designing for a new environment requires a lot of market research and delving deep for any information pertaining the area.
  • Giving clear instructions goes far when working with diverse teams. Not over-complicating tasks and providing templates helps a lot..
