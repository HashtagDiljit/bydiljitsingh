Green Hydrogen Plant (IDP2) — Concept & Detailed Design

Co-led the mechanical design for a 6-person team, delivering concept and detailed design of a green hydrogen facility near Melbourne, powered by renewable electricity and targeting ≥14,000 kg/day H₂ with carbon-zero operation.

Team size: 6 Role: Group Coordinator, Mechanical Design Duration: Feb–Apr 2024 Tools: Fusion 360, Ansys Fluent, Granta Edupack

Context & Challenge

Design a scalable, carbon-zero green hydrogen facility to supply the Melbourne region. The site is sited by the Patterson River to secure process water, with renewable electricity supply and space for future expansion. The core challenge was to meet a production target of ≥14,000 kg/day H₂ while integrating safe operations, high-purity gas separation, and practical storage/dispatch.

Objectives & KPIs

Production

Target ≥ 14,000 kg/day hydrogen with 24/7 runtime.

Purity

Gas separation to ~99.6% H₂ (PSA), compliant with end-use specs.

Safety

PFD/P&ID with interlocks, relief & emergency shutdown; storage up to 350 bar.

Sustainability

Renewable power (hydro/solar), water treatment & recycling, carbon-zero operations.

My Role & Contributions

As Group Coordinator for the mechanical team, I led coordination across sub-units and delivered key technical work: AWE electrolysis design, gas-separation module and storage inputs, PDS, sizing calculations, CAD for AWE & gas separation, and the integrated P&ID. I also contributed to the concept-stage business case, risk & sustainability work, and the final presentation.

“From concept to detailed design, I owned the AWE & gas-separation path and integrated it cleanly into the PFD/P&ID.”

  • 6 teammates coordinated across electrolysis, separation, storage & integration
  • Delivered 2 reports (concept + detailed), 1 PFD & 1 P&ID, plus final presentation
  • Maintained interface control — zero missed deadlines in the mechanical workstream

Technical Contributions

Electrolysis (AWE & SOE)

Defined PDS and sizing for low-temperature AWE and high-temperature SOE to meet the daily production target. Calculations for throughput, cell count/area, and energy were compiled into the detailed report and validated against runtime and temperature constraints.

AWE/SOE unit drawings & surface-area sizing study.

Gas Separation

Configured a multi-stage purification: ceramic membrane pre-purification, PSA to ~99.6% H₂, and a metal-hydride recovery loop to boost yield from dilute streams. For O₂, a PEM module achieved the required oxygen output and purity for storage/dispatch.

Gas separation train with PSA and recovery loop.

Storage & Compression

Specified gaseous storage at up to 350 bar using an array of cylinders (≈80 units), with thermal checks and power demand for compression. Defined controls and valve classes (motor valves, HP ball valves, JT throttling for cryo loop) for safe operations.

350 bar cylinder array and compression scheme.

Integration: PFD & P&ID

Authored the integrated PFD and P&ID covering pre-processing, electrolysis, cooling, separation, compression and storage with interlocks, relief, and ESD interfaces; sized utilities and defined key tie-ins for future expansion.

PFD/P&ID integration across the facility.

Reflections & Learnings

  • Interfaces matter: early, disciplined PFD/P&ID ownership prevented downstream clashes.
  • Purification yield improves markedly with staged recovery from dilute streams.
  • Storage/compression power and heat rejection are often under-scoped — size them early.
  • Documented PDS and traceability kept the team aligned under time pressure.
